Flower recommendations for Halloween

White Chrysanthemums

Chrysanthemums are the flowers of November, so it is impossible
to not start with them.
A huge variety of colours will help you find your favourite, but the
white ones carry much more meaning: They are the flowers of the
graveyards.
White flowers in general have a meaning of purity and spirituality too,
so it’s the best choice to have in a flower arrangement.

Orange roses

Of course we cannot leave out the most known flowers, Roses!
Luckily we can choose our favourites, for Halloween we
first would like to recommend the orange coloured ones,
carrying the meaning of brightness and pride and passionate romance.
With Jack-o-lanterns it’s the perfect choice for colour-harmony!

Black roses

Black roses have a much more different meaning compared
to their orange siblings, and yet they are still very close.
The meaning behind these flowers can go back to the word
“endings”, in a negative way it’s the end of a relationship or career,
in a positive way it’s rebirth and new beginnings!
With the orange ones it’s the best combination for Halloween.

Dracunculus Vulgaris

Dracunculus vulgaris has a lot of names, but the most usied is
probably snake or stinky lily or dragonwort.
With it’s purple flowers and overall dark colors it got the obvious
meaning according to the Victorian flower language books: Horror.
This flower, in contrast with the others, shouldn’t be kept inside,
because it has a strong rotting meat smell, that brings flies to it,
who will help the pollination.

Red Spider Lily

Red Spider Lilies are one of the most common flowers when you
think about autumn, graves, Halloween in Japan.
These poisonous flowers are growing mostly next to river banks and
they used them to keep the mices and rodents away from the farms.
They also have a huge mythological history behind them: In the
Buddhist Lotus Sutra they are the flowers that will lead you to your
next life in reincarnation.
Mostly they can be found on graves, as it is a tribute to the dead.
