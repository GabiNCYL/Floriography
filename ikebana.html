Ikebana

Beautiful flowers do not always make beautiful ikebana

Ikebana is the Japanese art of flower arrangement.
Ikebana or kado (way of flowers) started in the
Heian-period (794-1185) and still an important
part of the japanese arts.

The three classical Japanese arts of refirement:

  • Kado - Ikebana, the way of flowers
  • Chado - Tea ceremony, the way of tea
  • Kodo - The art of appreciating the incense

These artforms together affect all 5 senses,
sight, smell, touch, taste and hearing.

Purpose

The purpose of Ikebana is suprisingly not to create
decorations.
Instead it is about to learn how to appreciate certain
things that people normally ignore.

Rules

The 4 principles of Ikebana:

  • Approach
  • Movement
  • Balance
  • Harmony

With this your Ikebana will make you
also fresh and collected, as you can
learn from the Nature.

Elements

Ikebana has only 3 elements:

  • Line
  • Colour
  • Mass

Create the arrangement as an essence
of Nature.
Mainly all Ikebana has one flower and
one branch emphasized.
